Welcome to my home page!

I am an astrophysics PhD student at Harvard, with an interest in data science and machine learning.

My main scientific focus is on the stellar populations of galaxies: what kinds of stars are in a galaxy, and what can this tell us about how the galaxy formed? My past Astronomy Research is described under the Experience tab (link to the left).

In use high-performance computation to advance my research. I am pursuing a secondary sepcialization in Computational Science and Engineering as part of my PhD, which focuses on machine learning and data science. Highlights from my past Computational Projects are listed under the Experience tab.

The Experience tab also lists my Professional Activities, which highlights my engagement in science communication, teaching, and outreach.

You can also view my Academic CV and Resume, as well as a list of my Publications. I am always happy to talk to anyone who is interested in my work. Please feel free to Contact Me by email, Twitter, or LinkedIn.
